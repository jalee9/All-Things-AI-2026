# Major League Hacking AI Roadshow Session at All Things AI 2026
## 🧑‍💻 Key Takeaways & Notes for Students

## Context
The MLH (Major League Hacking) AI Roadshow session focused on how learning to build is changing in the age of AI. Instead of emphasizing memorization or traditional classroom learning, the session highlighted hands-on creation, experimentation, and using AI as a tool to bring ideas to life. These notes capture what matters most for building skills and standing out today.

---

## 🚀 Learning by Doing > Learning by Watching
- You won’t become a developer just by sitting in class  
- The real learning happens when you **build things**
- Projects, experiments, and trial-and-error matter most

---

## ⚡ Ideas Matter More Than Code
- AI can help you write code quickly  
- What stands out now is:
  - Your **ideas**
  - Your **creativity**
  - Your **ability to solve problems**

---

## 🤖 You’re Not Just Coding—You’re Directing AI
- Developers today don’t just write code  
- They **guide AI tools to build solutions**
- Key skills:
  - Writing good prompts  
  - Reviewing AI output  
  - Improving results step-by-step  

---

## 🧠 What Actually Makes You Stand Out
Hackathons (and real-world work) focus on:

- Communication  
- Creativity  
- Problem-solving  
- Learning quickly  
- Adapting when things don’t work  

---

## 🌎 More People Can Build Than Ever Before
- Millions of developers exist today  
- But now, **anyone can start building with AI**
- This means more opportunity—but also more competition

---

## 🔁 The Best Way to Learn Tech
- Technology changes constantly  
- The only way to keep up is to:
  - Try things  
  - Build projects  
  - Learn as you go  

---

## 🎯 What Employers Are Looking For
- Not just grades or assignments  
- They want to see:
  - Projects you’ve built  
  - Problems you’ve solved  
  - How you’ve used AI effectively  

---

## 💡 Bottom Line
- Don’t wait until you “feel ready”  
- Start building now  
- Use AI as a **tool**, not a shortcut
