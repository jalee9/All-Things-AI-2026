## 1. Framing the Conference

All Things AI is focused on how people are **actually using AI today**—not future ideas, but real-world applications.

- Professionals across industries are using AI daily  
- Students and developers are building real tools with AI  
- AI is already changing how work gets done  

👉 **What this means for you:**

> The skills you build now with AI will directly impact your future opportunities.

---

## 🧠 2. The Biggest Shift: Prompting → Context → Systems

### What most people think:
- AI is about asking good questions  

### What is actually happening:
- AI is about **providing the right context and structure**

### Key Idea:
> AI works best when you clearly define the problem and give it the right information.

### In simple terms:

| Old Way | New Way |
|--------|--------|
| Ask a question | Design how AI solves the problem |
| One answer | Iterative process |
| Focus on wording | Focus on context |

---

## 🔁 3. The AI Agent Loop

**Observe → Think → Act → Reflect**

### What this means:

Instead of:
- Asking AI once and stopping  

You should:
- Understand the problem  
- Guide the AI  
- Review the result  
- Improve it  

👉 **Real skill = using the loop, not just getting an answer**

---

## ⚡ 4. The Physics of Attention

AI performs best when your input is balanced.

- Too little → AI guesses  
- Too much → AI gets confused  
- Just right → best results  

👉 **Your job is to focus on what matters most**

---

## 🔁 5. The Repetition Tax

AI does not remember past conversations automatically.

- You often need to re-explain things  
- Poor context leads to poor results  

### Key Idea:
> The problem is not the tool—it’s how the information is structured.

---

## 🧱 6. Context Engineering

This is one of the most important skills you can learn.

It means:
- Giving AI the right information  
- Removing unnecessary details  
- Setting clear expectations  

### Simple breakdown:

- What does AI need to know?  
- What does it NOT need?  
- What are the rules or constraints?  

---

## 🤖 7. AI Agents

AI is moving beyond simple tools.

New systems can:
- Break problems into steps  
- Use tools  
- Improve over time  

### What this means:

> You are not just using AI—you are guiding a system.

---

## 👩‍🏫 8. Human in the Loop

AI can generate answers—but it still needs you.

You are responsible for:
- Checking accuracy  
- Making decisions  
- Improving results  

### Key idea:
> AI increases your ability—but also your responsibility.

---

## 🔥 9. What This Means for You

### To succeed with AI, you need to:

- Think clearly about problems  
- Provide better input  
- Evaluate output carefully  
- Keep improving your work  

### What matters now:

- Problem solving  
- Critical thinking  
- Communication  
- Adaptability  

👉 Not just memorizing steps or syntax

---

## 🧪 10. Try This

Use AI on your next assignment and follow this process:

1. Ask AI for help  
2. Improve your prompt by adding context and details  
3. Test the result  
4. Fix what’s wrong  
5. Reflect on what AI missed  

👉 The goal is not just to get an answer—it’s to improve how you think.

---

## 🎯 11. Closing Insight

> AI is not here to do the thinking for you.  
>  
> It is here to support your thinking.  
>  
> The students who learn how to guide AI, question it, and improve its output  
> will have a significant advantage in school and in their careers.

---

## 🌱 12. Philosophy

AI does not replace thinking—it redistributes it.

This resource is built on the belief that:
- You should learn how to structure problems  
- AI should support your thinking—not replace it  
- Your judgment is more important than ever
