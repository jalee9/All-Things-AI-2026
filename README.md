## 1. Framing the Conference 

All Things AI is not a hype conference—it’s intentionally built for practitioners applying AI in real environments.

- 80+ speakers across 6 tracks (builders, engineers, users, executives, governance, enterprise)  
- Focus: *“what’s working right now”* in real organizations  
- Heavy presence from companies like Meta, AWS, IBM, GitHub, Netflix  

👉 **Translation for faculty:**

> This wasn’t theoretical AI—it was how people are actually working with AI today.

## 🧠 2. The Biggest Shift: Prompting → Context → Systems

### What we *thought* we were teaching:
- Prompt engineering  
- “Write better instructions”  

### What the field is actually doing now:
**Context Engineering + Agent Systems**

### Key Insight:
> AI performance is no longer about the prompt—it’s about the environment you design around the model.

### In simple terms:

| Old Model | New Model |
|----------|----------|
| Ask AI a question | Design the system AI operates in |
| One prompt → one answer | Continuous loop of reasoning |
| Focus on wording | Focus on context + structure |

---

## 🔁 3. The AI Agent Loop (This is the Teaching Breakthrough)

**Observe → Think → Act → Reflect**

### Why this matters for education:

AI is no longer:
- “generate and submit”

It is:
- **An iterative thinking partner**

### Classroom Translation:

Instead of:
- One-and-done assignments  

Shift to:
- Iterative workflows:
  - Analyze problem  
  - Plan with AI  
  - Generate solution  
  - Critique and refine  

### 🔑 Key takeaway:
> “We should be grading the loop, not just the output.”

---

## ⚡ 4. The Physics of Attention (CRITICAL Insight)

- Transformer models scale with **O(n²) complexity**  
- More context ≠ better results  
- More context = **noise + cost + confusion**

### The “Goldilocks Zone”:
- Too little → hallucination  
- Too much → overwhelm  
- **Just right → high-signal thinking**

### Teaching Translation:
> Students don’t need more information—they need better-curated information.

---

## 🔁 5. The Repetition Tax (Why Students Struggle with AI)

- Every session starts with **zero memory**  
- Users repeatedly re-paste context  

### Insight:
> This is not a prompting issue—it’s a **context management issue**.

### Classroom Opportunity:

Teach students to:
- Build reusable context  
- Structure inputs  
- Think in systems, not prompts  

---

## 🧱 6. Context Engineering (The Skill We Should Be Teaching)

> “Curate and maintain the optimal set of information for AI reasoning.”

### The 3 Moves:

1. **Curate**
   - What belongs?  
   - What is noise?  

2. **Maintain**
   - Keep context relevant and updated  

3. **Structure**
   - Use tools, memory, and system prompts  

### Teaching Shift:

Instead of:
- “Write a good prompt”

Teach:
- “Design the conditions for success”

## 🤖 7. AI Agents = The New Mental Model

AI systems now include:
- Reasoning models  
- Tools  
- Memory  
- Multi-step workflows  

### Key idea:
> AI is no longer a tool—it’s a system that acts.

### Implication for students:

They need to learn:
- Task decomposition  
- Tool selection  
- Iteration strategies  

---

## 👩‍🏫 8. Human in the Loop (The Sweet Spot)

AI agents:
- Can act independently  
- But rely on:
  - Goals  
  - Rules  
  - Human oversight  

### Key perspective:
> “AI increases autonomy—but it also increases the need for human judgment.”

### Classroom framing:

We are not:
- Replacing thinking  

We are:
- **Elevating thinking**

---

## 🔥 9. What This Means for Our Courses (Actionable)

### Immediate shifts faculty can make:

### 1. Redesign Assignments
- From: final product  
- To: **process + iteration**

### 2. Teach the AI Loop
Have students explicitly:
- Observe  
- Think  
- Act  
- Reflect  

### 3. Reduce “Prompting” Activities
Replace with:
- Context-building exercises  
- Constraint design  
- Input evaluation  

### 4. Introduce “Too Much vs Too Little” Thinking
Help students:
- Trim inputs  
- Focus attention  
- Prioritize signal  

### 5. Require Reflection

Not:
- “What did AI give you?”  

But:
- “Why did this work or fail?”

## 🎯 10. Closing Insight

> “The biggest shift I saw at this conference is that AI isn’t about getting answers faster—it’s about thinking differently.  
>  
> Our role as educators is no longer just teaching students how to code or use tools.  
>  
> It’s teaching them how to structure problems, guide intelligent systems, and critically evaluate outcomes.  
>  
> In other words—we’re not teaching students to use AI.  
> We’re teaching them how to think with it.”
